Sample Answers Band 6.5–7.0

Part 1 — Traditional vs Modern

1. Are you interested in traditions and modern life?

Yes, to some extent. I would not say I am an expert, but traditions and modern life affects daily life, so I find it worth thinking about. For example, when I read news or talk with friends, this topic often comes up naturally.

2. Is traditions and modern life important in your country?

Yes, definitely. In my country, traditions and modern life matters because it influences families, young people, and the wider community. It may not be something people discuss every day, but it has a real impact on quality of life.

3. Has traditions and modern life changed in recent years?

Yes, quite a lot. Technology, education, and modern lifestyles have changed the way people think about traditions and modern life. Older people may have more traditional views, while younger people tend to be more open and practical.

4. Do young people care about traditions and modern life?

I think many young people do care, especially when it affects their future directly. However, they may express interest through social media or online discussions rather than traditional conversations.

Part 2 — Cue card sample

A traditional festival in my country

I would like to talk about a traditional festival in my country, which is closely connected to traditions and modern life.

I first became aware of it a few years ago, and since then it has stayed in my mind because it reflects both the positive and negative sides of modern life.

The positive side is that it shows the value of identity, family connection, and culture. This is important because people often need practical examples, not just abstract ideas, to understand a topic clearly.

At the same time, it also reminds me of the problem of traditions disappearing or becoming commercial. That makes the topic more complex, because there is rarely a perfect solution.

Overall, I find it meaningful because it helps me think more deeply about society and my own choices. If I had to summarise my feeling, I would say it is both interesting and relevant to everyday life.

Part 3 — Discussion

1. Why is traditions and modern life important?

It is important because it affects individuals and society at the same time. For example, identity, family connection, and culture can improve peoples quality of life, while traditions disappearing or becoming commercial can create long-term problems. That is why this topic deserves more attention from both citizens and governments.

2. What are the advantages related to traditions and modern life?

The main advantages are connected to identity, family connection, and culture. If this area is managed well, people can benefit through better opportunities, more convenience, and a higher standard of living. It can also help communities become more organised and forward-thinking.

3. What are the disadvantages or risks?

The biggest risk is traditions disappearing or becoming commercial. If people ignore this issue, it can lead to social pressure, inequality, or environmental damage depending on the situation. In my view, the negative side should not be exaggerated, but it should be taken seriously.

4. What should governments do about traditions and modern life?

Governments should create clear policies, invest in public education, and make sure rules are enforced fairly. For example, they can fund useful programmes and raise public awareness. However, government action alone is not enough; individuals also need to change their habits.

5. Do you think traditions and modern life will change in the future?

Yes, I think it will continue to change, mainly because of technology, globalisation, and younger generations. People are becoming more aware of both benefits and risks. In the future, I expect more practical solutions rather than simple arguments for or against it.

6. Do older and younger people think differently about traditions and modern life?

Yes, in many cases. Older people may value tradition, stability, and personal experience, while younger people are often influenced by online information and global trends. Neither side is completely right or wrong; the best approach is to combine experience with fresh ideas.
